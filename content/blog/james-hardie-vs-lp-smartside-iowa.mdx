Siding decisions in Iowa are rarely simple. Home exteriors here have to stand up to freeze-thaw cycles, hail, big temperature swings that can run from below -20°F in winter to above 100°F in summer, and moisture patterns that shift quickly from snow to rain to humid heat. On top of that, homeowners and builders sometimes have to think about impact damage, repainting expectations, and even woodpecker activity in certain locations.

Two of the most common premium siding conversations in Iowa come down to James Hardie and LP SmartSide. Both are respected products. Both perform well when installed correctly. But they solve the siding problem in different ways, and the better choice often depends on the project type, the install crew, and the homeowner's priorities.

## Why Iowa climate is tough on siding

Iowa siding does not get to coast. Water can work into small details and then freeze. Hail can test corners, lap edges, and trim. South- and west-facing walls can see long sun exposure during the hottest part of the year. Wind-driven rain and dramatic shoulder-season temperature swings add even more stress.

That is why material composition matters. Weight, moisture behavior, impact resistance, paint adhesion, and install precision all affect how siding looks five or ten years after the job is done.

## James Hardie: fiber cement built for durability

James Hardie HardiePlank siding is a fiber cement product, and that composition is one of its biggest selling points in the Iowa market. Fiber cement is known for durability, fire resistance, and strong long-term stability once it is installed correctly. It also tends to appeal to homeowners who want a solid, substantial exterior product with a reputation for performance.

James Hardie is especially attractive when hail resistance and finish durability are top concerns. Certain assemblies can align with Class 4 impact-rated roofing strategies elsewhere on the home, and homeowners often like the way Hardie holds paint and supports a crisp, architectural finish. It is also a product that carries weight in the marketplace because homeowners recognize the brand and often ask for it by name.

Beisser is an authorized James Hardie dealer, which matters because siding projects often involve more than lap boards. Trim, accessory coordination, installation guidance, and branch support can all influence how smoothly the job goes.

The main tradeoff is weight. James Hardie is heavier than engineered wood siding, which affects handling, cutting, and installation labor. Crews comfortable with fiber cement often like the end result, but it is not typically the fastest or lightest system to install.

James Hardie is often strongest when:

- The homeowner values fiber cement composition.
- Fire resistance and durability are important talking points.
- A premium painted finish is a priority.
- The crew is experienced with heavier siding systems.

## LP SmartSide: engineered wood with a different install profile

LP SmartSide approaches the siding challenge differently. Instead of fiber cement, it uses engineered wood strand technology. That gives the product a lighter feel during handling and installation while still targeting durability, paint performance, and impact resistance.

That lighter weight is a major reason many Iowa builders and remodelers like LP SmartSide. It is often easier to move, cut, and install than fiber cement, which can help with labor efficiency and crew comfort, especially on projects where schedule and handling speed matter. LP SmartSide is also treated to resist fungal decay and is often selected by homeowners who want a durable painted siding system without the added heft of fiber cement.

LP SmartSide holds paint well, and it performs strongly in many Iowa applications where impact resistance and practical install speed matter. Because the boards are easier to work with, some crews find it to be a more forgiving product when project logistics are tight.

LP SmartSide is often strongest when:

- Install speed matters.
- The builder wants a lighter product than fiber cement.
- The project needs strong curb appeal without the same handling burden as Hardie.
- The homeowner wants a durable painted siding option with impact resistance.

## Side-by-side comparison for Iowa projects

### Weight and installation

This is one of the clearest dividing lines. James Hardie is heavier and more labor-intensive to handle. LP SmartSide is lighter and often faster for crews to install. That does not automatically make LP better, but it does affect labor planning and crew preference.

### Price point

Exact pricing depends on profile, trim package, color strategy, and current market conditions, but Iowa homeowners will usually find that both products sit above entry-level siding options. Project totals can shift based on labor differences as much as raw material price. Because Hardie is heavier and more demanding to install, total installed cost may land differently than a homeowner expects from board pricing alone.

### Paint performance and finish expectations

Both products are known for holding paint well when installed and finished correctly. James Hardie has a long-standing reputation for clean painted exteriors. LP SmartSide also performs well on painted projects and remains a strong fit when homeowners want a sharp appearance without moving to fiber cement.

### Hail and impact performance

Both products can make a strong case in Iowa, where hail is always part of the conversation. James Hardie often gets attention for its fiber cement durability and impact-resistance positioning. LP SmartSide is also known for impact resistance and can be a compelling option for homes exposed to regular weather events.

### Moisture handling

James Hardie and LP SmartSide both require proper flashing, clearance, and installation detail work. Hardie's fiber cement composition appeals to buyers thinking about long-term stability. LP SmartSide counters with treated engineered wood technology and a lighter install profile. In practice, the better moisture performer is often the one installed with the better detail discipline.

## What Iowa contractors usually lean toward

Iowa contractors do not all choose the same winner, but there is a pattern. For new construction where labor efficiency matters and crews want lighter handling, LP SmartSide is often an attractive fit. For homeowners seeking a premium fiber cement exterior, or for projects where James Hardie's brand reputation is part of the value proposition, Hardie often leads the conversation.

For retrofit and remodel projects, the answer can depend heavily on crew familiarity and the condition of the existing wall assembly. Some remodelers prefer LP SmartSide because of easier handling and practical jobsite speed. Others lean toward Hardie when the homeowner specifically wants fiber cement or when impact resistance and perceived durability are central to the sale.

## Which one is right for your Iowa home?

Choose James Hardie when the homeowner wants fiber cement, values fire resistance, likes the long-term reputation of the brand, and is comfortable with the heavier install profile that comes with it.

Choose LP SmartSide when install efficiency matters, the project team wants a lighter product, and the goal is a durable painted exterior with strong impact performance and easier handling on the jobsite.

Neither answer is generic. The right fit depends on the crew, the house, and what the homeowner is actually prioritizing. A real siding quote should look at the entire assembly: lap or panel selection, trim, soffit, accessory details, and the building-envelope products behind the finish.
